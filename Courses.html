Marinduque State University
Courses

College Of Engineering


The School of Engineering was originally known as Marinduque School of Arts and Trades, and was expanded to include a ladderized Civil Engineering curriculum with 44 graduates in 2003. This ladderized curriculum was enhanced in 2008 to include additional skills subjects, allowing students to graduate with a one-year Building Electrician Course or a Diploma in a two-year Industrial Electrician course or a three-year Automation Technician course. Additionally, students can opt to graduate under a single-year Associate program in Civil Engineering or Electrical Engineering.

College Of Art And Social Sciences

The School of Arts and Sciences or SAS, primarily aims to strengthen instruction in General Education (GE) subjects and to offer Arts and Sciences – related courses. After undergoing several changes over the years since its establishment in 1995, the present-day School of Arts and Sciences is now home to students specializing in three different fields.

College Of Industrial Education

Marinduque State University School of Industrial Technology was established in 1952 as part of the Marinduque School of Arts and Trades. Over the years, the school evolved and underwent several organizational changes. In 1975-76, it was authorized to offer a four-year Bachelor of Science in Industrial Technology Curriculum. In 2005-06, it became a separate school, offering a two-year craftsmanship curriculum, a three-year technician program, and a four-year Bachelor's degree. In 2020, the Industrial Technology program became a separate school again after a period of being merged with the School of Information and Computing Sciences. Graduates of the program are expected to have strong technical skills and a sense of ethical and social responsibility

College Of Information And Computing Sciences

The School of Information and Computing Sciences (SICS) is a school focused on producing competitive IT professionals and IT-enabled individuals. It was established in 1998 and offers Bachelor of Science in Information Management and Bachelor of Science in Information Technology degrees. SICS has contributed significantly to the fulfillment of its mandates in Instruction, Research, Extension, and Production. The school has produced thousands of IT professionals, and its faculty and students are engaged in research and community projects. SICS is committed to providing quality education and public service.
